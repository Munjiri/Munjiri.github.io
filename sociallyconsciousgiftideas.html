SOCIALLY CONSCIOUS GIFT IDEAS

From some of the amazing people and organisations we've collaborated with this year.

Here is a list of creative and thoughtful presents, which do good. Making the person you're giving to super happy and  also the person who made the gift! 

Tea and coffee from Ajiri

From the top of Manga Hill, you can see the misty, fertile hills of Kisii, in western Kenya. Dotted with homes and bright green small holder farms, this is where you will find Ajiri's tea growing.

Ajiri creates employment for women in western Kenya, through the making of their handmade banana leaf labels. All profits from the sale of Ajiri Tea are donated to the Ajiri Foundation, which school fees for orphans in western Kenya. 

Ajiri Tea have an amazing variety of tasty teas and coffees and you can check out the video we made for them here and their site here.

Jewellery from Soul Design

Flames turn to red liquid, and that liquid turns to brass, and after some polishing a beautiful piece of Soul Jewellery emerges. We documented the process behind Soul's bespoke range of jewellery, chatting with designer Abi and artisans Fred and Francis.

Soul supports sustainable production and follows ethical sourcing of each element used in their collection. They encourage individuality, allowing you to create your own personalized look by combining and layering your favourite pieces and charms. Check out the video we made here and their site here.
A trip to Mushroom Farm

Mushroom farm is a social enterprise and eco-lodge, which believes in responsible tourism, sustainable community development, and local empowerment. Mushroom farm is situated on the edge of Nyika National Park (check out our other post) so has amazing views and they are also a great bunch!

If you are in Malawi, treat your friend or family to this amazing trip. If you aren't in Malawi, why not plan a trip to a place like this near you. We travelled to Mushroom Farm in July, check out the video here and their website here. 
Kids toys from Heartfelt

When we arrived in rural Makapanstad we were welcomed by Martha to her lovely home, behind which is Pinky Place, the workshop where all the Heartfelt magic happens. Here they employ 18 ladies, who hand make all their creations from scratch, beautifully sewn with precision. 

As well as products for events and occassions Heartfelt make a range of felt toys which would be perfect for the little people you have to buy presents for, see the video here and their website here.


Prints from Isla Rustrick


Isla creates a range of quirky prints and paintings. She recently finished working on a series which supported orangutan conservation projects. She also drew our about page image and works on personalised comissions. So if you have unique idea for a friend or family member let her know! See her instagram here. 


Make a donation to the Zambia Carnivore Project

If the person is adamant that they don't need anything, why not donate the money you would spend on a gift to a charity doing amazing work. Think about the issues that really matter to this person and find a cause that you know they would want to support.

We love the Zambian Carnivore Project. They are found in  a number of Zambia's awe inspiring National Parks such as Kafue, Liuwa and South Luangwa, protecting large carnivores and the ecosystems they live in through science, action and local leadership. See their website here.


Recycled Paper Books from Dedza Handmade

Between the rocky outcrops of the Dedza district is Dedza town just on the border of Mozambique. Here you will find Henry N'gambe and his beautiful recycled paper products such as photo frames, books and cards. His business is founded on a belief that local communities benefit environmentally and economically when re-using their local resources. His shop is also home to his brother's beautiful paintings. 

Find out more from his website here and watch our video here.

Jewellery from Acacia Creations

Acacia Creations create beautiful handmade jewellery and homewares. They were founded as a means to connect the skills of remote artisans with global consumers and believe in creating long-term employment, that cultivates empowerment and permanent change. 

We met with the Acacia Creations team in Kisumu, Kenya and documented the making of their handmade beads from recycled magazines. Some of the ladies even travel from Uganda to make them!

You can check out the video here and their site here.
