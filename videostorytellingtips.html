Normal, Explosion, New Normal. Traditionally we have been told a good story has to have a beginning a middle and an end. To be honest I don’t find that very helpful. A different way of looking at it is to think about "Normal, Explosion, New Normal”.

So lets begin with normal, this is how your characters life was at first. Then the explosion, this is something bad that happened or the conflict, something that they had to overcome. Then the new normal is how things turned out in the end. 

An easy way to think about it is the Lion King. Life was sweet for Simba living in the pride lands, life was normal, then bam! The explosion, the death of Mufasa, this was a conflict, a situation he had to deal with. Then finally the new normal, peace was restored to the pride lands.

Ok let’s say you’re selling an experience, such as an amazing trip to a National Park to someone, how would you create the story? Firstly what is normal? The normal is their everyday life going to work. The explosion or conflict is they're spending a lot of time working at their computer, they haven't had a break and it's stressing them out. The new normal is that once they've taken this amazing trip they feel better, they're connected with nature they feel less stressed they've realised their passion for wild flowers and the trip gave them time to spend with their family. So your service helped the character over come the explosion or conflict and in the end of the story, we have the positive, new normal. 

Focus on people not the product. It's important to focus on people not your product. You should add life and humanity to your message and make it relatable. Showing the people behind your business adds a human side and helps build a relationship. It's also important to choose compelling characters, who are comfortable with the cameras presence.

Stick to your key messages. Simplicity equals impact, more is not more. Whatever your product or service, you are likely to be going into a crowded market place and the way to stand out is to speak clearly about a few things. If you feel like you're being repetitive and telling the same story again and again, don't worry. You're the only one who sees all your posts, blogs, videos so being a bit repetitive looks like consistency and helps you become known for the thing you want to be known for.

Amazing visuals. Your compelling story needs amazing visuals, which match the story in order to prove your story. Think about the parts of your business that are visually interesting and focus on them, is your business inspired by the beautiful scenic location you are in, do you work with something colourful, do you source your raw materials from an interesting place? Is there an interesting process behind what you make?
