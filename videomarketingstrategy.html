VIDEO MARKETING

So you've made an amazing video, what should you do next? Here are a few video marketing tips to help you get the most out of putting your video online and promoting it.

free simple web templates
Name your video before you upload it. Give your video file a name, include an accurate description about the videos topic and some keywords. Good keyword sites include, answerthepublic.com and ubersuggest.com

Make sure your thumbnail image is captivating. With so many things trying to get peoples attention, make sure your thumbnail looks inviting and gets their attention. You can upload a still from the video or use a photo, which is what we tend to do as it looks high quality. Obviously your thumbnail should relate to the video, otherwise people will just click off.

Get that video on your site. Videos keep people engaged and keep them on your website, Google likes people spending longer on a site and refers to it as “dwell” time. It’s also great to get people to interact with your site rather than other just your social media accounts because who knows, instagram could become unpopular and then you’ve lost all those people you worked so hard to get as your followers.
Remember to add a meta title and meta description. This is not as complicated as it sounds, use keywords in your meta title and meta description, this is important in getting your video high on googles rankings. It’s effective and doesn’t take much time at all. 

Use transcription. Google can’t listen to your video and pick out the words being said (yet) so it's a good idea to have a transcription eg. the words that are being said in the video so that it can learn what your video is about and give you a higher chance of being ranked. 

Share your video across a lot of channels. Facebook, Twitter, Instagram are all good places which you probably already know, but they also encourage shares and shares create backlinks which Google loves ! Also when sharing your video on these platforms it’s a good idea to reupload the video directly, not share a link from youtube or vimeo. I think this is because facebook wants people to stay on facebook not get distracted by videos on youtube or vimeo. 

Use your video offline too. Your video doesn't have to only live online, you can use it at a trade show or to introduce a presentation you are giving.
