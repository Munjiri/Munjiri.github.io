HOW TO RECORD HIGH QUALITY AUDIO FOR YOUR VIDEO ON A BUDGET

Tips and equipment


1. Shoot in a quiet place, background sound can ruin your audio as it's so distracting. Listen out for humming sounds, close the windows and get away from traffic noises. It's interesting how our brain seems to able to ignore these sounds, but your mic will pick them up. 

2. Good acoustics and no echo. To test the echo or reverb of a room clap as loud as you can, if the reverb is bad you'll hear the clap echo, if you record here the voices will sound far away and muddy. The less you hear the echo the better the room is for recording.

3. Donâ€™t speak quietly, speaking loudly and clearly makes every mic sound better and is also free. If you absolutely do not want to spend any money, record your audio separately using the sound recorder on your phone, keep it close to you, do your narration and then add it to your video footage. 

4. To be honest your cameras in built mic and your phone mic aren't great. So you will have to spend a bit of money on a shotgun mic or a lav mic, if you want high quality audio. A shotgun mic is good for interviews whilst a lav is good if you have to move around a lot. Shotgun mics capture more of a range of sound, where as a lav is more focused.

5. Having a recording of a minute or so of background/ambient sound is also very helpful in an edit. For example you may have a gap in the person talking and you would use the ambient sound there. It's also useful to capture sounds that are specific to your film for example if you're filming someone making jewellery, capture the pings of the hammering.

6. If you are using a shotgun mic keep it as close as possible to the person you are trying to record, the further away you are the poorer the quality of the sound. 

7. Aim your audio levels to be at -12db and never go over -6db. You can use a zoom h4n to monitor your levels and do a sync clap eg. Clap your hands for matching up the audio and video later. 

8. Recording outdoors in wind is very difficult because of the crazy sounds that the wind makes on your mic. You can buy a wind protecter to minimize this effect and apparently you can also use a sock, but I've never tried this.
