How To Film Cinematic B-roll

How to create sweet b-roll through good planning, creativity, composition, lighting and by not rushing.

Ok firstly what is B-Roll? B-roll is supplemental or alternative footage intercut with the main shot. Basically the footage you put over your main shot.

Plan

Plan your shoot and what kind of b-roll you are going to capture, think about your storyline and how you are going to capture it. Where are you going to shoot? Sometimes you might not have much say in this, but if you do, pick something as visually interesting as possible. However always make sure it relates, unrelated b-roll can be really distracting and people who are watching your film will tune out. 

Also consider which equipment and lenses will help you enhance your story. It's always good to be super comfortable with your equipment, keep practicing and learning new things, before an important project.

Coverage

Filming in sequence is also helpful, make sure to get a range of wide, medium and close up shots, so you have enough footage when you go to edit. You will never have enough b-roll, the more b-roll you have the nicer edit and the more engaging the story. It's also going to make it a lot easier for you when you are editing as you won't be struggling to find the right shot. 

Be Creative

After you've got your basic shots, try different angles, different movements, gimbal shots, slow mo, shots with a shallow depth of field and aerials (try and get depth into these). I also think colour is really important to look out for. Make sure to look at other photographers and filmmakers / videographers work which you love and then figure out why you do! 

Composition

This can help you with your creativity and is one of the easiest ways to make your films look way better without costing you anything. There a number of composition rules that it's worth having a look at such as rule of thirds, centred composition and symmetry, foreground interest and depth, frame within a frame, leading lines, patterns and textures, filling the frame and leaving negative space.

Lighting

Light makes such a difference to any video or photos you create. Shooting in the early hours of the day eg. sunrise or just before the sun sets will let you catch the magical golden hours. Another thing to take into consideration is backlight and side light which can highlight your subject in such a beautiful way. Also look out for interesting reflections and shadows created by your light source.

Don't Rush

I think it's a good idea to prioritise your b-roll, don't say ah that's good enough. If it doesn't look amazing then switch it up, think about a different composition, colour, create movement, take some steps back and use some beautiful light! If you rush you'll be stuck in the edit stage and it won't be your best work.
